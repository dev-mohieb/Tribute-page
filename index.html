Carl Sagan

The Man who Sought the Stars

Here's a timeline of Carl Sagan's life:

  • November 9, 1934.

    • Born in Brooklyn, New York.

  • 1956.

    • Earns Bachelor's and Master's degree in Physics.

  • 1960.

    • Earns a Doctorate in Astronomy.

  • 1962 - 1968.

    • Worked at Harvard University and the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory.

  • 1968.

    • Became the director of Cornell University's Laboratory for Planetary Studies.

  • 1971.

    • Became a full profesor at Cornell University.

  • 1973.

    • Published The Cosmic Connection: An Extraterrestrial Perspective.

  • 1978.

    • Was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for his book The Dragons of Eden.

  • 1980.

    • Cofounded the Planetary Society.

  • 1983.

    • Cowrote the paper that introduced the concept of the “nuclear winter”.

  • 1984.

    • Coauthered The Cold and the Dark: The World After Nuclear War.

  • December 20, 1996.

    • Died in Seattle, Washington.
